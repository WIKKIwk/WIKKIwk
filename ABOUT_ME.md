# About Me

I am a neutral person by nature. I do not spend much energy trying to control how people see me. I would rather let the work speak first. If someone looks at the projects and values them, good. If someone critiques them, that is useful too. I do not feel forced to prove that I am senior or exceptional. The systems should speak for themselves, and I can always improve the next version.

## What Pulls Me In

Outside of writing code itself, I am deeply interested in ERP systems, especially the architecture behind them. I enjoy logic, structured thinking, and questions that feel like puzzles. That interest is probably connected to why subjects like mathematics and geometry used to attract me so much. ERPNext is interesting to me for exactly that reason: it is not just software, it is a logic system for how a business moves.

## How I Think About Skill

I do not like to describe myself with claims about strength or level. Strength is always relative, and there will always be people who know more. I am still early compared to the systems I respect most, and I know I am far from complete. What matters more to me is not claiming ability, but building things that make the result visible.

If I had to describe the pattern behind my work, it would be this:

- minimal effort, maximum result
- simpler operation for the user
- less explanation required from the builder
- interfaces that remain usable even for the least technical operator

I believe good software should not assume an ideal user. It should work for the most confused user too. If the system can be used clearly by the weakest operator in the loop, then stronger users will move through it naturally.

## What I Am Building

The bigger goal of the ecosystem is not just to have an ERP. I want a business owner or factory operator to see where each product moved, what workers did, how much work happened, what kind of forecast is forming, and what the operation looks like right now. I want that to be visible through strong mobile interfaces, clear middleware layers, and systems that hide unnecessary ERP complexity from the teams that should not need to know it.

In that vision:

- ERPNext is the business core
- middleware hides complexity
- mobile delivers clean execution
- hardware becomes part of the workflow
- AI helps guide and explain operations

## Collaboration

The kind of work that fits me best is industrial automation, product building, and AI-driven systems. I am most engaged when the product has to connect real business logic, real workflows, and real operational constraints.
